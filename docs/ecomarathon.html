Overview

The successful execution of this project relies on a structured framework of strategic planning, financial oversight, and strict adherence to competition standards.

To fund the procurement of necessary materials and components, we are securing sponsorships with a maximum budget of €850.

Planning and Preparation
At the project's inception, we are establishing clear role definitions and completing essential preliminary work. Our initial discussions and strategic planning cover the following areas:
• Vehicle Specification: Determining the specific type of vehicle and its overall design aesthetic.
• Material Selection: Identifying the most suitable materials for each individual component.
• Chassis and Configuration: Finalizing the number of wheels and the specific wheel configuration.

Compliance and Safety
To ensure eligibility for the race, we have conducted a thorough review of the official competition regulations. This includes:
• Establishing comprehensive safety protocols.
• Ensuring all design and construction aspects comply with the official competition requirements.

Model

Vehicle Configuration

The model utilizes a three-wheel configuration, featuring two wheels at the rear and one at the front. As illustrated in the figure above, this layout was specifically chosen to ensure optimal stability, as the driver operates the vehicle from a reclined position. This configuration provides the necessary balance while the vehicle is stationary, a level of stability that would not be achievable with an inverted wheel arrangement.

Frame Material: Carbon Fiber Carbon fiber was selected as the primary material for the frame. Its high strength-to-weight ratio significantly reduces the overall mass of the vehicle, enhancing both acceleration and energy efficiency.

Body Composition: Fiberglass The vehicle body is constructed from fiberglass. This material was chosen for its lightweight properties and its versatility, allowing it to be easily molded into complex, aerodynamic shapes.

Simulation and Analysis The entire design was simulated using Siemens NX software. These simulations accounted for the combined weight of the driver and the batteries to accurately predict maximum deflection and ensure structural integrity.

Results

The body features a streamlined, aerodynamic design engineered for optimal performance. To comply with safety regulations requiring emergency egress within 10 seconds, a gap was intentionally integrated between the front and rear sections of the body.
This structural modification ensures the driver can exit the vehicle rapidly and safely during an emergency.
